The Polish language and its features

Polish is a Western Slavonic language with about 40 million speakers mainly in Poland.
There are also significant Polish communities in many different countries.
Polish is closely related to Kashubian, Lower Sorbian, Upper Sorbian, Czech and Slovak.

The Alphabet

Linguists argue that there are no languages more difficult the others, because the children learn them equally easily. However, Polish is definitely very particular. The alphabet does not differ widely from the one you know as an English speaker, but it does have some particular distinctions of some of the letters.
